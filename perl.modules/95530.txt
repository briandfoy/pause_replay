I wrote a blog post a few years back, where I tried to be more specific =

http://neilb.org/2013/07/24/adopt-a-module.html =
<http://neilb.org/2013/07/24/adopt-a-module.html>

My executive summary would be:
Raise an issue on the dist saying (a) what it is you=E2=80=99d like =
changed, and why; and (b) explicitly offer to =E2=80=9Chelp out=E2=80=9D, =
if given co-maint.
If there=E2=80=99s a repo, do a pull request (if you get to where =
you=E2=80=99re asking the PAUSE Admins for co-maint, you can refer to =
this and say =E2=80=9Chere=E2=80=99s my repo, where I fixed X and Y=E2=80=9D=
)
If neither of the above works, send an email to the author, cc=E2=80=99ing=
 modules@perl.org <mailto:modules@perl.org>. Explicitly ask for =
co-maint, and say that if you haven=E2=80=99t heard anything in a month, =
then you=E2=80=99ll ask the PAUSE admins to give you co-maint.
If you get no reply to that, or it bounces, have a look for other email =
addresses: sometimes they=E2=80=99re a different address will be listed =
in the AUTHOR section of the pod. Look for her or him on LinkedIn, =
google them, email to module-authors@perl.org =
<mailto:module-authors@perl.org>
Two weeks after that email, try again, if you have a working email =
address.
A month after the original email where you cc=E2=80=99d the PAUSE =
admins, send email to the PAUSE admins, cc=E2=80=99ing the author, =
asking for co-maint and outlining which of the above steps you did. Ie =
demonstrate due diligence.
